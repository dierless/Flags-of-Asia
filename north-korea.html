North Korea

Specifications

  • Local Name: 조선민주주의인민공화국 (Chosŏn Minjujuŭi Inmin Konghwaguk)
  • Proportion: 1:2
  • Name of the Flag: 람홍색공화국기 (Ramhongsaek Gonghwagukgi)
  • Adopted: September 8, 1948

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • Red: Revolutionary spirit, patriotism, and socialism
  • Blue stripes: Sovereignty, peace, and friendship
  • White: Purity, unity, and the Korean ethnic identity

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • Red star: Communism, leadership of the Workers’ Party, and guiding ideology
  • Central red horizontal band with white borders and blue outer stripes

Meaning / Special Display

  • The flag is most prominently displayed during large-scale mass games, military parades, and national holidays such as the Day of the Foundation of the Republic.
  • It often appears alongside leadership imagery, symbolizing loyalty and unity with the state.
  • Military units use distinct variants with emblems, but the national flag itself does not have alternate civil versions.

Description

  • The flag represents North Korea’s socialist values, national identity, and political ideology.
  • For its citizens, it signifies unity, loyalty, and devotion to the nation and its leadership.
