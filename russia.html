Russia

Specifications

  • Local Name: Российская Федерация (Rossiyskaya Federatsiya)
  • Proportion: 2:3
  • Name of the Flag: Государственный флаг Российской Федерации (Gosudarstvennyy Flag Rossiyskoy Federatsii)
  • Adopted: December 11, 1993

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • White: Nobility, honesty, and the aspiration for peace
  • Blue: Loyalty, faithfulness, and the Virgin Mary (a historic protector figure in Russian tradition)
  • Red: Courage, strength, and the resilience of the Russian people

    • Shapes / Symbols:

  • Horizontal tricolor: Represents historical unity among the Russian lands and the evolution of the Russian state

Meaning / Special Display

  • Displayed prominently during national celebrations such as Russia Day (June 12) and Victory Day (May 9).
  • Commonly paired with military symbols during parades, representing national strength and remembrance of historical battles.
  • Russian naval forces use a separate naval ensign (St. Andrew’s Flag), but the national tricolor is used for civil and state purposes.

Description

  • The flag reflects Russia’s long historical continuity, cultural pride, and emphasis on unity across its vast territory.
  • For many Russian citizens, the tricolor symbolizes patriotism, endurance, and a deep cultural connection to tradition and identity.
  • It represents the values of perseverance, national strength, and loyalty to the homeland.
