South Korea

Specifications

  • Local Name: 대한민국 (Daehan Minguk)
  • Proportion: 2:3
  • Name of the Flag: Taegeukgi (태극기)
  • Adopted: January 27, 1883

Symbolism

    Colors:

  • White background: Peace and purity
  • Red and blue circle (Taegeuk): Balance of the universe (yin and yang)
  • Black trigrams: Elements of heaven, earth, water, and fire; harmony of opposites

    • Shapes/Symbols:

  • Central Taegeuk circle divided into red (upper) and blue (lower)
  • Four black trigrams, one at each corner, each representing a natural element and a philosophical principle

Meaning / Special Display

  • The flag represents balance, harmony, and the fundamental principles of the universe according to Eastern philosophy.
  • Used in official events, it symbolizes the unity and identity of the Korean people.

Description

  • The Taegeukgi embodies South Korea’s values of peace, harmony, and balance in life.
  • For its citizens, it signifies national pride, cultural heritage, and unity.
